Mira Manalastas (she/they) is a printmaker from Washington, DC currently working in Charlottesville. Their work investigates and exposes the continuing effects of US imperialism, feudalism, and bureaucrat capitalism in the Philippines.

About

Through violence, fear, and humor, they subvert a beloved fast-food mascot, Jollibee, and force him to exist in the current political and social climate occuring in the Philippines. Heavily influenced by the legacy of protest in printmaking history, the immediacy and clarity of making protest signs, and the accessibility and empowerment found in street art and graffiti; Miraâ€™s work seeks to engage their viewer in deciphering the contexts they force Jollibee to exist in and to reflect on how the broad themes of US imperialism occur in their daily lives. Mira works mainly with monotype, relief, and newsprint to emphasize immediacy instead of preciousness.

Work

I work mainly with monotype, monoprints, relief, and utilize crayons and ink in creating additional layers.
